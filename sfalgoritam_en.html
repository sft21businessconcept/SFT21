Example of Fund Distribution

Introduction

The SFalgorithm for member networking and fund distribution is based on a binary matrix model.

Below is one of many mathematical examples of how the SFalgorithm functions, assuming that EVERY NEW MEMBER;

  • starts their business by paying for the first starter package - SP1 (€50)
  • pays for at least the first three (3) consecutive subscription periods (SSP1, SSP2, SSP3)
  • studies the business concept during the first subscription period (SSP1)
  • enrolls their first direct member during the second subscription period (SSP2)
  • enrolls their second direct member during the third subscription period (SSP3)

It is important to note that this is a mathematical example that is almost impossible to replicate in practice.

Specifically, if every new member started with the first starter package (SP1 / €50), enrolled their first direct member in the second subscription period (SSP2 / €50), enrolled their second direct member in the third subscription period (SSP3 / €50), and then neither made new upgrades nor enrolled new members, according to the laws of mathematics, duplication, and multiplication, the results would be identical to the distribution shown below...

However, the company cannot and does not wish to prohibit members from enrolling new members in the first accounting period, nor can it dictate how many new members will be enrolled in the first, second, or any subsequent subscription period.

But, knowing from practice that many members will start their business with one of the higher-value starter packages for better benefits... knowing that there will be a large number of members who will immediately start registering new members, that many will have three, four, or many more direct members, while there will be those who will not build a career... for these very reasons, it is almost impossible to predict the speed of user growth or the potential earnings.

Thus, the distribution example shown below is truly just an example and cannot be taken as a model for expected business growth or expected earnings.

However, the given example shows the real value and power of our business concept, according to which almost anyone can afford to start a business with a deposit of up to €150 over the first five months.
